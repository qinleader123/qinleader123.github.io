Quick Facts

Died: February 18, 1546, Eisleben, Germany
Education: University of Erfurt (1501–1505)
Spouse: Katharina von Bora (m. 1525–1546)
Children: Margaretha Luther, Hans Luther, Paul Luther, Magdalena Luther, Elisabeth Luther, Martin Luther

Overview

Martin Luther, born November 10, 1483 in Eisleben, Germany was a professor of theology, priest, author, composer, and who is best known as the reactionary of the prodestant reformation causing the division of Western Christendom between Roman Catholicism.

Early Life

Luther was born on November 10, 1483 in Eisleben, Saxony (now Germany). The next morning he was baptised on feast day of St. Martin of Tours. One year later, he and his family moved to Mansfield (10 miles northwest) where his father prospered in the copper refining business. Luther's father later became a town councilor. Luther had many brothers and sisters and he is known to have been close to one of them named Jacob.

Education

Luther's father was determined to see Luther become a lawyer so to start he sent him to a Latin School in Mansfield. Later Luther went to a school in Magdeburg operated by the Brethren of the Common Life, he later compared his life there to hell. In 1501, at the age of 17 he entered the University of Erfurt, where he took a course in liberal arts and received a baccalaureate degree in 1502. In 1505 he earned his masters degree. Luther took a course in law but dropped out shortly after because he belived law represented unsureness. On July 2nd, 1505, on his way back to the university a lightning bolt struck near him and in fear he yelled "Help! Saint Anna, I will become a monk!" He took his cry as a vow and left the university for the St. Augustine's Monastery in Erfurt on July 17th, 1505.

The Reformation

In 1517, as a professor of moral theology at the University of Wittenberg Luther wrote the 95 Theses. The 95 Theses attacked the Catholic Church's sale of Indulgences. The 95 Theses also put forward two beliefs, 1. that the Bible is the central religious authority and 2. that humans may reach salvation only by their faith and not by their deeds. Copies of the 95 Theses made their way around Germany and the rest of Europe. In July of 1520, Pope Leo X issued a public decree that said Luther's writing was heretical and forced Luther to recant in Rome, Luther refused to recant so the Pope excommunicated him from the Catholic Church. In April of 1521 Luther appeared before the Diet of Worms, after refusing to recant once again he concluded with the statement "Here I stand. God help me. I can do no other." In May, the Holy Roman emperor Charles V ordered Luther's writings to be burned. By late 1521 the reform had grown beyond Luther's control and it started getting more political; leaders of nations started leading the reform.

Marriage

In 1523, Luther helped smuggle 12 nuns out of the Nimbschen Cistercian convent in herring barrels. Later in 1525 Luther married Katharina von Bora, one of the nuns he smuggled out. They had 5 children, Margaretha Luther, Hans Luther, Paul Luther, Magdalena Luther, Elisabeth Luther, and Martin Luther Jr.

Final Years

By 1531 Luther had been suffering from many health issues such as Ménière's disease, vertigo, fainting, tinnitus, and a cataract. The years leading up to his death in 1546 his health had deteriorated even futher where he developed kidney and bladder stones, athritis, and angina. Luther's last journey was to Mansfield because of his concern for his sibling's families still continuing Luther's father's copper refining business. They were being threatened by Count Albrecht of Mansfield who wanted to take the industry under his own control. The negotiations regarding the industry were concluded on February 17th, 1546. Later that night Luther died from a stroke.
